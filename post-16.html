Fear Not Death But Defeat

April 18, 2025

In my journey as an L2 Support Engineer at NetOne Zambia, I have faced many challenges that tested my resilience. But some of the most enduring lessons about perseverance came from my childhood in Chingola, Copperbelt Province. Growing up next door to my friends Lusa Kabemba and Lweendo Kabemba, we shared a bond that taught me a profound truth: it is not death we should fear, but defeat. In this post, I reflect on those early days and the courage we found in standing together, a lesson that continues to shape my life and career.

Lessons from a Childhood Bond

In Chingola, where the grass swayed under open skies and neighbors felt like family, Lusa, Lweendo, and I were inseparable. We lived next door to one another, sharing laughter, scraped knees, and dreams whispered under the shade of trees. Those moments were just play, they were our first battles, teaching us to stand up again no matter how many times we fell. To fear death is to dwell on what none can escape, but to fear defeat is to dread the moment you stop fighting, stop believing, stop standing shoulder to shoulder with those who matter most.

The Strength of Togetherness

Our childhood was filled with small defeats, missed games, tough days, or the sting of a scolding, but what mattered was the choice to rise again. Lusa and Lweendo were my allies in those adventures, reminding me that strength is communal. It is in the clasped hands, the shared glances, the unspoken promise to keep going together. That lesson, learned on a simple path in Chingola, carries into my work at NetOne, where teamwork and perseverance drive our success in supporting complex IT systems.

Reflection: A Moment Frozen in Time

Final Thoughts

Lusa, Lweendo, and I may have grown up and taken different paths, but the lessons from our childhood remain. Fear not death, for it is a path we all walk. Fear defeat, for it is the shadow cast by giving up. As I navigate the demands of my career at NetOne, I am grateful for those early days in Chingola, where I learned that standing tall with friends makes any challenge surmountable. Let us honor those bonds and let our lives echo the courage of youth, when the world was wide, and we faced it together.
