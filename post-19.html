April 21, 2025

Looking back at my journey as an L2 Support Engineer at NetOne Zambia, I often find myself reflecting on the moments that shaped my passion for technology. One such memory takes me back to 2021, during my time at the National Institute of Public Administration (NIPA) in Lusaka, Zambia. A Day in the Computer Lab

The "Eat Cookie" Challenge

That day, our lecturer assigned us an interactive task called the "Eat Cookie" activity. The objective was simple yet insightful: create a button that, when clicked, changes a cookie emoji from sad to smiling and displays a toast message saying Cookie eaten on the screen. It was a small exercise, but it taught us the fundamentals of event handling, UI interaction, and Android development. I remember the satisfaction of getting the button to work, seeing the emoji change, and the toast message pop up, small victories that fueled my passion for coding.

Lessons Beyond the Code

Those lab sessions at NIPA were more than just coding exercises; they were building blocks for my growth as a developer and problem-solver. Every line of code written, every bug fixed, and every success message displayed was a step forward. But beyond the technical skills, these moments taught me the importance of perseverance and humility. The challenges of debugging, the late nights studying, and the collaborative spirit of the lab shaped my perspective, reminding me to work hard on my craft, stay consistent, and remain humble, no matter how far I progress in my career.

Carrying the Mindset Forward

Now, as an L2 Support Engineer at NetOne, I carry the lessons from NIPA with me. The same dedication I applied to the "Eat Cookie" activity is what I bring to solving complex IT challenges today. Whether I am troubleshooting network issues or configuring systems, I am reminded of the computer lab at NIPA, where I learned that growth comes from embracing the process, celebrating small wins, and staying grounded. This throwback is a testament to how foundational experiences shape our journeys, both as professionals and as individuals.
